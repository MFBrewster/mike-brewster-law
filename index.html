Estate Planning: Questions and Answers

What does estate planning do for me?

A professionally drafted will, power of attorney, health care proxy, and trust dramatically eases the difficulty family members must encounter when faced with the loss of a loved one.

Why not use a form from LegalZoom?

While form documents are usually better than having no documents at all, only an experienced attorney will be able to evaluate your unique circumstances, identify potential problems or pitfalls, and recommend courses of action to accomplish all of your goals with minimal difficulty for you and your loved ones. A form which is not fitted to your unique circumstances could potentially do more harm than good.

Is it difficult?

Not especially. A comprehensive plan must account for all of your personal and financial circumstances and needs, with backup plans in case of any changes. A typical estate plan can be completed in two meetings of thirty to sixty minutes. You should expect to spend a total of perhaps four hours' time spread out over a month or two.

Is it expensive?

A typical married couple can expect to get wills, powers of attorney, health care proxies, and (if they wish) living wills for less than a thousand dollars. The initial consultation is free of charge.
